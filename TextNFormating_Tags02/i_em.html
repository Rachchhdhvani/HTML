I/EM Tag

Details:

 * Both <i> and <em> make text italic, but they have different meanings:

üîπ <i> ‚Äî Italic for visual style only
* Used to visually differentiate text.
* No special importance or meaning.
* Non-semantic (purely decorative).
* Common Uses: Foreign words, technical terms, book titles, etc.

üîπ <em> ‚Äî Emphasized (important) text
* Adds semantic meaning ‚Äî the text should be read with emphasis.
* Screen readers may stress or change tone when reading..
* Semantic HTML element.

Syntax of I/EM Tag

He said <i>carpe diem</i> and walked away.

You <em>must</em> attend the meeting today.
